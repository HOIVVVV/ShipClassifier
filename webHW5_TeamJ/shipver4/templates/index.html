Introduction to Underwater Acoustic Noise

Passive Sonar is a technique used to detect and analyze underwater sounds without emitting signals. It involves listening for sounds in the environment rather than sending out sound pulses. This project focuses on analyzing acoustic data to classify ship types and make predictions based on acoustic data uploaded.

Type of vessel and Their Locations

Filter by Vessel Type

  • Motor Boat: Significant contributors to noise due to large engines and continuous operation.
  • Ocean Liner Ships: Cruise ships and ferries produce substantial low-frequency noise.
  • Tanker: Generates noise primarily from high-powered engines and propulsion systems.
  • Trawler: Fishing equipment like trawl doors contributes to noise production.
  • Tug Boat: Dynamic positioning systems and high-power engines create significant noise.
  • Pilot Ships: Short-range vessels with high engine power that produce considerable noise.
  • Passenger Ships: Emit intermittent, high-frequency noise, especially in coastal areas.
  • Mussel Boat: Specialized vessels with equipment that generates moderate noise levels.
  • Cargo Ships: Large engines and machinery contribute to continuous, low-frequency noise.
  • RORO Ships: Roll-on/roll-off ships generate noise from engines and cargo handling operations.
  • Fish Boat: Fishing Vessel
  • Sail Boat: Generally quieter but may produce noise from auxiliary engines.
  • Natural Ambient Noise: Background noise from wind, waves, and marine life in the environment.

Sources and Factors Influencing Underwater Noise

Sources of Underwater Noise from Ships

  • Propeller Noise: Cavitation and blade rate frequency.
  • Engine and Machinery Noise: Vibrations and operations transmitted through the hull.
  • Hydrodynamic Noise: Turbulence from water flow around the hull and appendages.
  • Structural Noise: Vibrations resonating through the ship's structure.

Factors Affecting Underwater Noise

  • Ship Speed: Faster speeds increase cavitation and turbulence.
  • Ship Size: Larger ships generate more noise.
  • Propeller Design: Damaged or inefficient designs increase noise.
  • Engine Type and Placement: Diesel-electric engines reduce transmission.
  • Operational Environment: Shallow waters amplify noise.
